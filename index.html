Fall 2023 Classes


COMP 311: Computer Organization

The course covers the fundamentals of computer hardware design, transistors and logic gates, progressing through basic combinational and sequential components, culminating in the conceptual design CPU. (From the UNC Catalog)

Link to Computer Science Department Site


COMP 455: Models of Languages and Computation

Introduction to the theory of computation. Finite automata, regular languages, pushdown automata, context-free languages, and Turing machines. (From the UNC Catalog)

Link to Computer Science Department Site


COMP 562: Introduction to Machine Learning

Machine learning as applied to speech recognition, tracking, collaborative filtering, and recommendation systems. Classification, regression, support vector machines, hidden Markov models, principal component analysis, and deep learning. (From the UNC Catalog)

Link to Computer Science Department Site


COMP 590: Foundations of Software Engineering

Review of basic inference; two-sample comparisons; correlation; introduction to matrices; simple and multiple regression; analysis of variance; use of statistical software. (From the UNC Catalog)

Link to Computer Science Department Site


INLS 512: Applications of Natural Language Processing (Also COMP 486)

Explores current and future uses of natural language technologies. Topics vary and may include translation, generation, deception, health informatics, ethics and evaluation, and student-selected areas of interest. (From the UNC Catalog)
