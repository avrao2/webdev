Spring 2023 Classes


COMP 301: Foundations of Programming

Students will learn how to reason about how their code is structured, identify whether a given structure is effective in a given context, and look at ways of organizing units of code that support larger programs. (From the UNC Catalog)

COMP 211: Systems Fundamentals

The overarching goal is to bridge the gap between a students' knowledge of a high-level programming language and computer organization. (From the UNC Catalog)

Link to Computer Science Department Site


STOR 435: Introduction to Probability

Introduction to mathematical theory of probability covering random variables; moments; binomial, Poisson, normal and related distributions; generating functions; sums and sequences of random variables; and statistical applications. (From the UNC Catalog)

STOR 455: Methods of Data Analysis

Review of basic inference; two-sample comparisons; correlation; introduction to matrices; simple and multiple regression; analysis of variance; use of statistical software. (From the UNC Catalog)
